Yerba Mate (the traditional way)

Introduction

Not many drinks can offer the health benefits of tea, the strength of coffee, and the joy of chocolate like South America’s super brew, yerba mate. Containing roughly as much caffeine as coffee, about 80 milligrams per cup, mate has gained global popularity—much so that brands like Perrier, Red Bull, and PepsiCo have launched mate drinks. According to Future Market Insights, global yerba mate sales will total $2.18 billion in 2023, and in the next decade, demand for the elixir will rise by 5.7 percent. But long before the beverage reached German discos and American grocery stores, it was a traditional tea made from the steeped leaves and twigs of an indigenous plant, consumed across Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Paraguay, and Uruguay for its health benefits. Many South Americans continue to brew yerba mate using similar methods developed by their Indigenous ancestors. Along with supposed benefits of supporting weight loss, concentration, and better digestion, drinking yerba mate continues to symbolize culture and tradition in the Southern Cone.

Items Required:

  • gourd
  • bombilla
  • yerba
  • water @ room temp
  • water @ 70 - 80 degrees celsius

Traditional Mate Drinking Circle Preparation

  1. Fill yerba mate gourd/cup with 1/2 - 2/3 full of yerba.
  2. Cover the opening and shake it up and tilt it to a 45 degree angle to create a small mountain on one side.
  3. Pour over room/cold water to swell up the leaves leaving the mountain side dry.
  4. After 2-3 minutes, pour not boiling but hot water (70-80 degrees celsius) and enjoy the drink!
